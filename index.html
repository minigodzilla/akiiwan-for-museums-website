Akiiwan: The Museum Experience

Within museums, historical sites and other institutions around the world, Indigenous artifacts are all too often placed behind glass – creating a passive experience where the visitor can only see them.

Akiiwan for Museums aims to break these artifacts free from their glass confines, enabling visitors to interact with them in exciting new ways – reconnecting these artifacts to the communities that created them, and to the land where they were born.

With an attractive and easily repositionable dual-display kiosk including a touchscreen, visitors can virtually craft an object, making their own design and aesthetic choices, all while learning about the object's history.

They then get to take their custom creation home, in the form of a 3D augmented reality model, that they can continue to interact with, and learn from, using their own phone.

These models become part of an interactive tabletop diorama that connects Indigenous artifacts, histories and traditions from across the country and around the world into a cohesive, one-of-a-kind experience, personalized for the visitor.

With each museum you visit, your collection grows as you rebuild the rich history of Indigenous peoples around the world, their artisanry, culture and remarkable technology.
